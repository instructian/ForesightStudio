**Cal State East Bay International Co-Synchronous Classroom Initiative**

Dear Anthony,

Here is an update on our international co-synchronous classroom model, developed in collaboration with Mannheim University of Applied Sciences. This initiative demonstrates how a relatively simple approach to global education can yield remarkable enrichment through both virtual and physical exchanges.

### The Classroom Model

Our *Designing Futures* course convenes once a week, with students meeting simultaneously at **9:30 a.m. in California** and **5:00 p.m. in Mannheim**. This timing allows for a true co-synchronous exchange, where students experience the same class in two continents at once.

This year we have introduced an **alternating structure**:
- Plenary sessions where the entire class comes together to share findings and exchange ideas.
- Small-group sessions where mixed teams of Mannheim and CSUEB students collaborate while faculty circulate among the groups providing guidance and feedback.

One of the most exciting outcomes of the second iteration of the class is the creation of a **course book on speculative design**, developed collaboratively from last year's work. Students this year are already using it as a guide and shared reference.

The initiative includes:
- Two CSUEB graduate students studying in Mannheim.
- Three Mannheim students studying at CSUEB.
- Four Mannheim graduate students visiting CSUEB to experience the class both virtually and physically.

Faculty exchange has also strengthened the partnership through reciprocal thesis reviews by Professors Kai Beiderwellen and Ian Pollock.

Speculative design and strategic foresight provide students with methods for envisioning desirable futures while grounding those visions in research and actionable strategies for sustainability.

The program continues to evolve through action research and iterative refinement, strengthening assignments, collaboration methods, and student learning each year.

This initiative demonstrates a scalable model for international education in which sustained collaboration across continents becomes part of the normal rhythm of university life.
