# Product

## Register

product

## Users

- **Students (speculative design courses):** capture, explain, and connect weak signals during horizon-scanning assignments. Often first exposure to foresight methods; they need scaffolding, jargon-free labels, and confidence that their observations matter. Used in class and at home, on varied (often older) laptops.
- **Instructors / Administrators:** run a term: approve the roster, assign students to terms, curate submissions (verify Raw signals), watch class analytics, manage invite codes. Task-driven, time-poor, grading under deadline.
- **Subscribers (enterprise/consulting, later phase):** read-only consumers of verified signals and published scenario sets.

## Product Purpose

Foresight Studio is a collaborative strategic foresight platform bridging speculative-design pedagogy and enterprise strategy work. It moves users from raw horizon scanning to defensible strategic narratives: multi-dimensional assessment, semantic deduplication, a relational knowledge graph, and scenario planning. Success = a student can submit a well-formed signal (Emergent or Shadow) without training, and an instructor can run an entire term (approve → observe → verify → analyze) without touching a database console.

## Brand Personality

Rigorous · Collaborative · Calm. "An academic notebook crossed with a modern research workspace." Methodological depth carried by clear, jargon-free labels. The interface is quiet so the thinking is loud.

## Anti-references

- The generic SaaS dashboard: purple-blue gradients, hero-metric cards, identical card grids, glassmorphism (DESIGN.md's "Anti-Slop Pact").
- Spreadsheet-as-UI: the product exists because signal tracking in spreadsheets fails students.
- Over-decorated ed-tech: mascots, gamification chrome, confetti. Students are treated as researchers, not children.
- Strangeness without purpose: no reinvented form controls, no display fonts in labels, no decorative motion.

## Design Principles

1. **Scaffold, don't simplify.** Guide the user through rigorous method (STEEP, polarity, horizons) with progressive disclosure — never by removing the rigor.
2. **Provenance is sacred.** Sources, attribution, and merge history stay visible (Design Justice: shadow research must not be erased). Every claim traces to its origin.
3. **Shadows get equal light.** Decline/worst-case signals are a first-class capture path, not an afterthought toggle — preventing blind spots is a stated product goal.
4. **The tool disappears into the task.** Earned familiarity: standard affordances, consistent component vocabulary, density where analysts need it.
5. **State is always visible.** Raw vs Verified, approved vs pending, Emergent vs Shadow — status is legible at a glance and never relies on hue alone.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.2 AA: ≥4.5:1 body contrast (DESIGN.md's OKLCH ramps already target this), visible focus rings, full keyboard paths, `prefers-reduced-motion` alternatives on all animation. STEEP category tags and polarity/verification badges never encode meaning by hue alone (always paired with label or icon). Forms are screen-reader tested (label associations, error announcements).
